# HISTORY OF NARENDRA MODI

## Early Life and Background

**Birth and Family:**
- Born: September 17, 1950, in Vadnagar, Gujarat, India
- Parents: Damodardas Mulchand Modi (father) and Hiraben Modi (mother)
- Family Background: Born into a lower-middle-class family of the Ghanchi-Teli (oil-presser) community
- Siblings: Third of six children

**Childhood:**
- Helped his father run a tea stall at the Vadnagar railway station
- Described as an average student who was more interested in theater and debates
- Early interest in politics and public speaking

## Education

- Completed higher secondary education in Vadnagar
- Bachelor's degree in Political Science from Delhi University (1978) through distance learning
- Master's degree in Political Science from Gujarat University (1983)
- Known to have been influenced by nationalist literature and ideology during his formative years

## Early Political Career

**RSS Involvement:**
- Joined the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) at age 8
- Became a full-time RSS worker (pracharak) in 1971
- Served the RSS in various capacities during the 1970s and 1980s

**Entry into BJP:**
- Joined the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) in 1987
- Quickly rose through party ranks due to organizational skills
- Became General Secretary of BJP Gujarat in 1988
- National Secretary of BJP in 1995

## Chief Minister of Gujarat (2001-2014)

**First Term (2001-2002):**
- Became Chief Minister of Gujarat on October 7, 2001
- Initial appointment came after the resignation of Keshubhai Patel

**2002 Gujarat Riots:**
- Major communal riots occurred in February-March 2002
- Riots resulted in significant loss of life, primarily among Muslims
- Modi's handling of the riots became controversial and subject to extensive scrutiny
- Supreme Court-appointed Special Investigation Team (SIT) investigated allegations
- In 2012, SIT gave a clean chit to Modi
- Supreme Court upheld this decision in 2013

**Subsequent Terms:**
- Won elections in 2002, 2007, and 2012
- Served as Chief Minister for nearly 13 years
- Focused on economic development and infrastructure
- Promoted Gujarat as a business-friendly destination
- Launched initiatives like Vibrant Gujarat Summit
- Known for emphasizing governance and development model

**Economic Development:**
- Gujarat experienced significant industrial growth during his tenure
- Attracted domestic and international investment
- Improved infrastructure including roads, ports, and power supply
- Agricultural development programs
- Critics pointed to concerns about inclusive growth and social development indicators

## Rise to National Politics

**2014 General Elections:**
- Named BJP's Prime Ministerial candidate in September 2013
- Led aggressive campaign focused on development and governance
- Used social media and technology extensively
- Campaign slogan: "Achhe Din Aane Wale Hain" (Good Days Are Coming)
- BJP won historic mandate with 282 seats (first party to win majority since 1984)
- Became Prime Minister on May 26, 2014

## Prime Minister of India (2014-Present)

**First Term (2014-2019):**

*Major Initiatives:*
- Swachh Bharat Abhiyan (Clean India Mission)
- Make in India campaign
- Digital India initiative
- Jan Dhan Yojana (financial inclusion)
- Skill India program
- Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana (LPG connections)
- Goods and Services Tax (GST) implementation (2017)
- Demonetization (November 2016)

*Foreign Policy:*
- Proactive foreign policy with visits to numerous countries
- Emphasis on "Neighborhood First" policy
- Strong focus on diaspora engagement
- Enhanced strategic partnerships globally

**Second Term (2019-2024):**
- Won re-election with increased majority (303 seats)
- Sworn in on May 30, 2019

*Major Developments:*
- Abrogation of Article 370 (special status of Jammu and Kashmir) in August 2019
- Citizenship Amendment Act (2019)
- COVID-19 pandemic management (2020-2022)
- Vaccine development and distribution (Covaxin, Covishield)
- Ayushman Bharat health insurance scheme expansion
- Housing for All initiatives
- Infrastructure development focus

**Third Term (2024-Present):**
- Won 2024 general elections
- BJP did not secure absolute majority on its own but formed government with NDA alliance
- Currently serving as Prime Minister

## Key Policies and Programs

**Economic Initiatives:**
- Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme
- Atmanirbhar Bharat (Self-Reliant India)
- Startup India
- Mudra Yojana for small businesses
- National Infrastructure Pipeline

**Social Welfare:**
- Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (Save and Educate Girl Child)
- PM-KISAN (direct income support to farmers)
- Ayushman Bharat (health insurance)
- Housing schemes
- Rural development programs

**Technology and Innovation:**
- Digital payments and UPI expansion
- Aadhaar-based service delivery
- Smart Cities Mission
- Space program advancement

**Environmental Initiatives:**
- International Solar Alliance
- Climate change commitments
- Renewable energy targets
- Electric vehicle promotion

## Leadership Style

- Known for centralized decision-making
- Strong emphasis on personal branding and communication
- Active on social media with massive following
- Regular radio program "Mann Ki Baat" (since 2014)
- Emphasis on direct communication with citizens
- Focus on nationalism and cultural identity

## Recognition and Awards

- Time Magazine's Person of the Year (reader's poll) multiple times
- Various international awards and honors
- Honorary doctorates from multiple universities
- Global leadership recognition

## Controversies and Criticism

- 2002 Gujarat riots and his role
- Concerns about secular fabric and minority rights
- Press freedom and civil liberties debates
- Economic policies and their impact
- Handling of farmers' protests
- COVID-19 pandemic management criticism
- Demonetization impact debates

## Personal Life

- Married to Jashodaben Chimanlal Modi (estranged marriage from youth)
- Known for simple lifestyle and long working hours
- Vegetarian and teetotaler
- Practices yoga and meditation
- Interests in photography and writing

## Legacy and Impact

Narendra Modi remains one of the most influential and polarizing figures in contemporary Indian politics. His tenure has been marked by significant policy initiatives, economic reforms, and a distinctive approach to governance and foreign policy. Supporters credit him with strong leadership, economic development, and enhanced global standing, while critics raise concerns about democratic values, inclusive growth, and social harmony.
